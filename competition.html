📈 Scoring Rules

  • The scoring of each track is independent.
  • Track 1 will be evaluated by a mix of automated metrics, Track 2&3 will be evaluated by expert reviews.
  • Furthermore, Outstanding Participants will be awarded based on Track 2&3.

    • Track 1: Texture Restoration

    • Track 1 includes four tasks: Hybrid Camera Deblur, Deocclusion, Color Reconstruction and 3D Reconstruction.
    • For each task, multiple metrics and their weights will be announced, including reference-based and no-reference-based metrics.
    • Participants' score of each task will be based on the rank they get on the corresponding task, as listed below:
      • Rank 1st 2nd 3rd 4th 5th 6th 7th+
      Score 23 11 5 2 1 1 0
    • Participants's final score in Track 1 is the sum of the points they get on each task.
    • Tiebreaker: if two team get the same final score in Track 1, the team with fewer participants will be ranked higher.
    • For example, a team challenges three tasks on Track 1, and get 2nd on Hybrid Camera Deblur, 3rd on Deocclusion, 5th on Color Reconstruction, their final score is 11+5+1=17.

    Track 2: Dataset & Benchmark

    • A weighted ranking rule based on expert review scores for dataset contribution.
    • Datasets demonstrating strong generalization on real-world data are highly recommended.

    Track 3: Open-Spike Research

    • A weighted ranking rule based on expert scores for research innovation.
    • Proposals that inspire spike vision to explore new applications and stimulate future tasks are highly recommended.
